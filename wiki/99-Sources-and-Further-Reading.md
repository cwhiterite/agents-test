# Sources and Further Reading

This wiki summarizes and adapts established software design literature and public pattern catalogs. The pages are intentionally practical and AI-assistant oriented.

## Core Sources

- Robert C. Martin's SOLID principles are widely summarized as Single Responsibility, Open/Closed, Liskov Substitution, Interface Segregation, and Dependency Inversion. See TechTarget overview: https://www.techtarget.com/whatis/definition/SOLID-software-design-principles
- Gang of Four patterns come from *Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software* by Erich Gamma, Richard Helm, Ralph Johnson, and John Vlissides. A concise public overview is available from DigitalOcean: https://www.digitalocean.com/community/tutorials/gangs-of-four-gof-design-patterns
- Martin Fowler's *Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture* catalog: https://martinfowler.com/eaaCatalog/
- Gregor Hohpe and Bobby Woolf's *Enterprise Integration Patterns* catalog: https://www.enterpriseintegrationpatterns.com/eaipatterns.html
- Microsoft Azure Architecture Center cloud design patterns: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/architecture/patterns/
- Chris Richardson's microservices pattern catalog, including Saga: https://microservices.io/patterns/data/saga.html
- AWS Prescriptive Guidance on Strangler Fig modernization: https://docs.aws.amazon.com/prescriptive-guidance/latest/modernization-decomposing-monoliths/strangler-fig.html

## Recommended Books

- *Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software* by Gamma, Helm, Johnson, and Vlissides.
- *Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture* by Martin Fowler.
- *Enterprise Integration Patterns* by Gregor Hohpe and Bobby Woolf.
- *Domain-Driven Design* by Eric Evans.
- *Implementing Domain-Driven Design* by Vaughn Vernon.
- *Clean Architecture* by Robert C. Martin.
- *Refactoring* by Martin Fowler.
- *Microservices Patterns* by Chris Richardson.
- *Release It!* by Michael Nygard.
- *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* by Martin Kleppmann.
